Key Findings

Below is a summary of the most important issues identified in this audit, ranked by potential impact on your business.

1. Your Phone Number is Your H1 Tag Easy Fix

The homepage H1 tag is “(662) 281-8455” — a phone number, not a descriptive headline. Search engines use H1 to understand page content. 107 of 182 pages (59%) are missing H1 tags entirely. Fix the homepage H1 to something like “Oxford’s Premier Dental Care — Life Dental.”

Action Required:

Update the homepage H1 in your CMS to a descriptive headline. Then audit all service and location pages to ensure each has a unique, descriptive H1 tag.

2. Half Your Site is an SEO Ghost Major

The site serves duplicate content at www and non-www URLs, effectively creating two copies of every page. 100+ pages have canonical conflicts, duplicate titles, and duplicate meta descriptions. One server redirect rule would fix 60% of all SEO issues found in this audit.

Action Required:

Implement a 301 redirect in your server configuration (or CDN) to force all www.lifedentalgroup.com traffic to lifedentalgroup.com. This single change resolves canonical conflicts, duplicate titles, and duplicate meta descriptions across the entire site.

3. Mobile Site Takes 29 Seconds to Load Major

Mobile performance scored 37/100 with a 29.1-second Largest Contentful Paint (LCP). Google uses mobile speed for rankings. 1,601 KiB of unused JavaScript is being loaded on every page. Visitors on phones are leaving before the page finishes loading.

Action Required:

Audit and remove unused JavaScript bundles. Implement code splitting to load only what each page needs. Defer non-critical scripts. Target LCP under 2.5 seconds. This likely requires developer involvement.

4. 133 Pages Have No Meta Description Easy Fix

73% of the site has no meta description. These are the snippets Google shows in search results. Without them, Google generates its own — often poorly. Each missing description is a missed opportunity to control your search appearance and click-through rate.

Action Required:

Write unique, compelling meta descriptions (150–160 characters) for your top 20 service and location pages first. Include a call-to-action and relevant keywords. Then work through remaining pages by priority.

5. No Reviews or FAQ Schema for AI Search Moderate

The site has solid Organization and BreadcrumbList schema but is missing FAQPage and Review/AggregateRating markup. AI search engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity prioritize sites with rich structured data. Your FAQ pages exist but have no FAQ schema — and your 1,170 Google reviews are invisible to structured data.

Action Required:

Add FAQPage schema to your existing FAQ pages. Add LocalBusiness schema with AggregateRating to your Oxford location page. Add Review schema to showcase your 4.9-star rating in search results.

6. Your Competitors Are Closing the Review Gap Moderate

At 1,170 reviews you’re #3 in Oxford by review count. Stubblefield has 1,672 and Oxford Dental has 1,454. Both have higher or equal ratings. A review generation strategy could close this gap — you need approximately 500 more reviews to take the #1 position.

Action Required:

Implement a systematic review request process: post-appointment texts or emails with a direct Google review link. Set a goal of 20+ new reviews per month. Train front desk staff to mention reviews at checkout.

7. No Facebook Presence for Ads or Community Easy Fix

Your Facebook profile is private or inaccessible and zero Facebook ads are running. Instagram has 1,674 followers and 578 posts — solid. But Facebook remains the #1 social platform for dental practice marketing in the 35–65 demographic, where most dental spending decisions are made.

Action Required:

Make your Facebook business page public and active. Cross-post Instagram content to Facebook. Consider allocating a portion of your Google Ads budget to Facebook/Instagram ads targeting the 35–65 demographic in Oxford, MS.

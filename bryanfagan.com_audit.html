Executive Summary

The Law Office of Bryan Fagan operates a 185-page website focused on family law, estate planning, criminal defense, and guardianship services across the Houston/DFW Texas market. The site is actively supported by paid advertising through Google Ads (managed by Palms Media LLC) with 10 currently running ad creatives.

This audit identified critical technical SEO issues that are likely undermining both organic search performance and paid ad ROI. The most severe finding is a broken homepage title tag (827 characters of corrupted code), combined with duplicate homepage indexing, missing security headers across all 185 pages, and multiple pages where ad spend is driving traffic to technically flawed landing pages.

The homepage title tag is severely corrupted. Instead of the intended ~50-character title, it renders as an 827-character string filled with repeated "ionicons-v5-e" text — a clear sign of a broken icon font library leaking into the <title> element.

Duplicate Homepage

Both https://www.bryanfagan.com/ and https://bryanfagan.com/ return identical content (42,775 words each). The canonical tag points to the non-www version, but Google is indexing both, splitting authority between them.

Root Cause: Why the Site Is So Slow

1. Hero image is invisible to the browser preloader.

The homepage hero section uses a CSS background-image instead of an <img> tag. The browser cannot discover or preload CSS background images, so the largest visual element (LCP) doesn't start loading until after CSS is fully parsed — adding 3-4 seconds of delay.

2. Triple Google Tag Manager containers.

The site loads 3 separate GTM containers plus HubSpot tracking loaded twice. Each container can inject dozens of additional scripts. This is the primary driver of the 1,400ms+ Total Blocking Time.

3. 279 network requests, 6MB total page weight.

4. 68 render-blocking resources waste 3,460ms.

Before the page can display anything, the browser must download and process 68 CSS and JS files. This alone adds 3.5 seconds of white-screen time.

5. www → non-www redirect penalty.

Every visitor to www.bryanfagan.com gets redirected to bryanfagan.com, wasting 883ms – 2,335ms per page load. Since all Google Ads point to the www version, every ad click starts with this penalty.

Sales Call Talking Points

1. "Your homepage title tag is broken."
Instead of showing "Bryan Fagan Law: Texas Family & Estate Attorney" in Google, your homepage title is 827 characters of corrupted code. This is the #1 most important on-page SEO element, and it's broken on your most important page.
2. "You're paying for ads to pages that may not exist."
Three of your Google Ads landing pages (/family-law/divorce, /family-law/austin, /emergency/custody) weren't found when we crawled your site. If these are returning errors or redirects, you're burning ad budget on broken experiences.
3. "Google gives your site a 33 out of 100 for performance."
Your homepage scores 33/100 on Google's own PageSpeed test. Your largest content takes 7 seconds to appear (Google wants under 2.5). The page isn't interactive for 36-40 seconds on mobile. Potential clients searching from their phone are leaving before your page even loads.
4. "Every ad click wastes 1-2 seconds on a redirect."
All your Google Ads point to www.bryanfagan.com, but the site redirects to bryanfagan.com (without www). That redirect alone wastes 883ms to 2.3 seconds per visit. For someone clicking your emergency custody ad, that's an eternity.
5. "You're running 3 separate GTM containers and HubSpot is loaded twice."
Your site loads 98 JavaScript files totaling 2.5MB. Three Google Tag Manager containers and duplicate HubSpot tracking are blocking the page for over 1,400ms. Consolidating to 1 GTM container alone could save a full second of load time.
6. "Your site has zero security headers."
All 185 pages are missing Content-Security-Policy, HSTS, and Referrer-Policy headers. For a law firm handling sensitive client information, this is a liability risk and a negative trust signal to Google.
7. "15 pages are telling Google to look somewhere else."
You have 15 canonical URL conflicts where pages point Google to different URLs — including one that sends authority to an entirely different domain (texasdivorcelaw.net). This is bleeding your SEO value.
8. "Your pages average 31,000 words each."
A typical well-optimized page has 1,000-3,000 words. Your pages average over 31,000. This suggests a template issue loading redundant content on every page, which hurts load time, crawl budget, and keyword focus.

Executive Summary

Your website is serving duplicate content on both www and non-www domains.
Every page on your site exists at two separate URLs (with and without "www"), splitting your search engine authority in half. This single issue is responsible for 23 duplicate titles, 22 duplicate meta descriptions, and 29 canonical URL conflicts. Google is wasting crawl budget indexing the same content twice instead of ranking it once.
Your site has zero security headers — leaving visitors and your reputation exposed.
None of your 73 pages have Content Security Policy, HSTS, or X-Frame-Options headers. Modern browsers and search engines increasingly use security signals as trust indicators. Without them, your site is vulnerable to clickjacking and man-in-the-middle attacks.
No paid advertising while 3 of your 4 local competitors are running Google Ads.
Tate Accident Law, Bill Kennedy Law, and Brett Evans Law all have active Google Ads campaigns. Brett Evans has been running image ads since August 2022. Every day without ads, potential clients searching "personal injury attorney Denison TX" are clicking on your competitors instead.
Quick Win: Fix the www/non-www redirect Easy Fix
Add a single 301 redirect rule in your WordPress settings or .htaccess file to force all traffic to one version (either www or non-www). This one change will eliminate 23 duplicate titles, 22 duplicate meta descriptions, and 29 canonical conflicts instantly.
Quick Win: Fix duplicate H1 tags on blog posts Easy Fix
16 pages have two H1 tags — the site name "Bradshaw Coones, PLLC" appears as an H1 alongside the actual page heading. This is a WordPress theme issue that can be fixed by changing the site name element from H1 to a div or span in the header template.
Quick Win: Add missing meta descriptions Easy Fix
7 pages (mostly category pages) have no meta description at all. Adding unique, keyword-rich meta descriptions takes minutes per page and directly improves how your pages appear in search results.
